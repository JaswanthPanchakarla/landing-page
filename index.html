In the honoring memory of Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose is considered the most influential freedom fighter with extraordinary leadership skills. He believed that freedom is not given, but taken through sacrifice and unwavering courage.

The Path of a Patriot

The Great Escape. In 1941, disguised as a pathan, he escaped house arrest to seek global support. He traveled by foot and train to Kabul, proving no wall could contain a free spirit.

Supreme Command. He rebuilt the Azad Hind Fauj in 1943. It became a symbol of unity and heroism, inspiring thousands to join the struggle.

Unwavering Sacrifice. Netaji taught his troops that it is their duty to pay for liberty with their own blood. He demanded ultimate devotion to the cause.

The Rebel Leader. Even when he disagreed with others, he remained a man of integrity, driven by a singular goal: a sovereign India. Despite sharp differences, he famously hailed Mahatma Gandhi as the "Father of our Nation".

The Eternal Legacy. His slogans "Jai Hind" and "Delhi Chalo" electrified the masses. He empowered women by forming the Rani Jhansi Regiment, ensuring all classes fought for independence.

"Freedom is not given—it is taken. One individual may die for an idea, but that idea will, after his death, incarnate itself in a thousand lives."

- Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose

Champion of Indian Nationalism

Netaji's military strategy and assertive nationalism challenged an empire and paved the way for India's independence.
